The Secrets of the Tetragrammaton

An exploration of the esoteric framework

Based on the paper by "THE OFFICIAL URBAN"

Introduction

This presentation explores a numerological and esoteric framework that maps the Tetragrammaton (Yod-He-Vau-He) to the Tarot.

We will examine a recursive cycle of septenaries and regenerative sets to understand this divine blueprint.

Foundational Definitions

Three key concepts form the basis of this system.

1. Triangular Number (TA)

The ancients had a conception of numbers which is almost lost in modern times. The idea of the Unity in all its manifestations led to numbers being considered as the expression of absolute laws. This led to the veneration expressed for the 3 or for the 4 throughout antiquity, which is so incomprehensible to our mathematicians.

It is however evident that if the ancients had not known how to work any other problems than those we now use, nothing could have led them to the ideas we find current in the Hindu, Egyptian, and Greek Universities.

They are of two kinds: theosophic reduction and theosophic addition.

These operations are theosophic because they cause the essential laws of nature to penetrate throughout the world; they cannot be included in the science of phenomena, for they tower above it, soaring into the heights of pure intellectuality.

The Theosophical Addition of a number (X) is the sum of all integers from 1 to X. 


                    TA(X) = 1 + 2 + 3 + ... + X
    
                    Example:
                    TA(4) = 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 = 10

2. Reduction Number (TR)

The Theosophical Reduction of a number reduces it to a single digit by summing its digits recursively. 


                        Example:
                        TR(123) = 1 + 2 + 3 = 6
                        TR(99) = 9 + 9 = 18  ->  1 + 8 = 9

3. Active Manifest

A number (X) is an "Active Manifest" if its Triangular Number, when reduced, equals 1.

TR(TA(X)) = 1

This signifies a point of completion or regeneration in a cycle.

The Tetragrammaton Cycle (1-4)

The four-letter Hebrew name of God, YHVH (יהוה), encapsulates the fundamental process of creation.

The Four Qualities

  • Yod (י): Active / Male / Fire. The initiating spark.
  • He (ה): Passive / Female / Water. The receptive vessel.
  • Vau (ו): Neuter / Child / Air. The balancing connector.
  • 2nd He (ה): Active Manifest / Earth. The culmination and regeneration.

Active Regeneration

The cycle repeats, with each "Active Manifest" point starting a new cycle. This occurs when TR(TA(X)) = 1.

  • TR(TA(4)) = TR(10) = 1
  • TR(TA(7)) = TR(28) = 1
  • TR(TA(10)) = TR(55) = 1
  • TR(TA(13)) = TR(91) = 1

This pattern continues infinitely, with each manifest point becoming the 'Yod' of the next cycle.

Septenaries and the First Set (1-21)

A "Septenary" is a fundamental unit of seven numbers, embodying the Hermetic Law of Gender through two overlapping cycles.

Masculine & Feminine Cycles

A septenary contains a masculine (initiating) and feminine (nurturing) cycle, forming a balanced whole.

Masculine Cycle (1-4): An upward, active triangle (△). Represents the initiating force.

Feminine Cycle (4-7): A downward, receptive triangle (▽). Represents the generative vessel.

Defining the Septenary

Together, the two cycles interlock at the number 4, creating a symbolic "Star of David."

This structure represents the harmony of active and passive forces, culminating at 7, an Active Manifest point.

The First Set: A Trinity of Septenaries

The first full "Set" consists of 21 numbers, structured as a YHVH pattern of septenaries.

  • Yod: First Septenary (1-7)
  • He: Second Septenary (7-13)
  • Vau: Third Septenary (13-19)
  • 2nd He: A final regenerative step (19-21), culminating in the birth of the next set at 22.

[Image: The full first set, showing three hexagrams and a final triangle, arranged in the YHVH cross pattern.]

The 21 to 22 Jump

Three septenaries complete at 21 (3 x 7). The number 22 is a special point of regeneration.

21 is the completion (The World in Tarot).

22 is the new beginning (The Fool in Tarot), as TR(TA(22)) = 1.

This +1 jump represents the spark that turns an end into a new, higher-octave beginning.

Subsequent Sets

The entire pattern of 21 numbers repeats on higher octaves.

The Four Sets

The model outlines four complete sets, each spanning 21 numbers and ending with a +1 regenerative jump.

  • First Set: 1-21 (Regeneration at 22)
  • Second Set: 22-42 (Regeneration at 43)
  • Third Set: 43-63 (Regeneration at 64)
  • Fourth Set: 64-84 (Regeneration at 85)

This structure creates a total of 84 numbers, with 85 starting the next grand cycle.

Tarot Mapping

This numerological formula is used to lay out the entire 78-card Tarot deck.

The 22 Major Arcana represent the first set (1-21 + 0/22 for The Fool).

The 56 Minor Arcana are structured within this framework, divided into four suits (YHVH).

Manifestation in Nature & Systems

The YHVH pattern is a universal principle of creation, observable across many domains.

Active (Yod) + Passive (He) + Neuter (Vau) → Manifest (2nd He)

Examples

  • Atomic Structure: Proton (+) + Electron (-) + Neutron (0) → Atom
  • Family Dynamics: Father + Mother + Child → Family
  • Hegelian Dialectic: Thesis + Antithesis + Synthesis → New Paradigm
  • Musical Harmony: Root Note + Third Note + Fifth Note → Chord

Conclusion

The Tetragrammaton pattern reveals a universal law of creation where three forces interact to produce a fourth, manifest result.

This recursive structure is evident in nature, human systems, and esoteric traditions like the Tarot, reflecting an infinite spiral of evolution.
